The Problem

Global warming is acknowledged as one of the greatest challenges our society faces today.

According to PNAS, approximately 98% of all published climate scientists support the consensus on climate change.

Food production is the cause of one fourth of the planets CO₂ emissions. This includes all of the steps from initial production, transportation and packaging, before it ends up in your local store.

According to a study done by the WWF in Sweden called “One Planet Plate”, we have to adjust our weekly food intake to a sustainable level to not exceed 11kg CO₂-equivalents. If many people make small changes, the impact will be big.
There are currently no commercial tool which helps you track your own C02 emissions, when it comes to grocery shopping.

Therefore we conducted a survey to see if there is an interest among the common consumer for this service:


"I would like to know the amount
of emissions I produce"


We could tell from the survey that 78 % of the participants would be interested to see their own impact.

Therefore we developed a platform where the average consumer can track their emissions when shopping groceries. Read more about our solution
