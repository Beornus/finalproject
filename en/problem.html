The problem we are facing:
The public is not aware enough of how their grocery shopping choices impact the environment.
Image by: Unsplash

According to PNAS, approximately 98% of all published climate scientists support the consensus on climate change. Food production is the cause of one fourth of the planets CO₂ emissions. This includes all of the steps from initial production, transportation and packaging, before it ends up in your local store.

According to a study done by the WWF in Sweden called “One Planet Plate”, we have to adjust our weekly food intake to a sustainable level to not exceed 11kg CO₂-equivalents. If many people make small changes, the impact will be big. There are currently no commercial tool which helps you track your own CO₂ emissions, when it comes to grocery shopping. Therefore we conducted a survey to see if there is an interest among the common consumer for this service. Participants were asked if they agreed or disagreed to the following statement:


"I would like to know the amount
of emissions I produce"





We could tell from the survey that 78 % of the participants would be interested to see their own impact. Therefore we developed a platform where the average consumer can track their emissions when shopping groceries.
